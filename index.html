The Hub of Advancement

Welcome to Sanicon Valley, the one and only interplanetary future city, that lies on the great city of San Jose, far more advanced than many other cities in the world. Our city blends advanced technology with nature and repurposing waste for the greater good.

Sanicon Valley is home to more than 1,000,000 residents, as it is the hub of social, economic, and technological advancement, with the forward-thinking system of urban planning and repurposing waste. We fix the major problem which has never been solved for decades in San Jose: improving our farm to table system.

Vertical Agriculture

Sanicon Valley not only fixes the extreme water shortage but also uses it to keep plants healthy. We repurpose shower water to sustain vertical backyard farms. This ensures every house has a private grocery store in their own backyard.

Citizens use an app to order robotic delivery of fresh produce. Surplus notifications build a strong community bond that San Jose currently lacks, ensuring no food is wasted and even the poor can access nutrition.

Zero Footprint

We trap carbon dioxide for fizz drinks, dry ice, and oxygen conversion. This interplanetary solution converts waste into useful nutrients, energy, and fuel. Remaining food waste becomes compost, and cooking oil is repurposed into soaps.

Plastic-eating bacteria in special bins decompose waste immediately, leaving practically zero net waste. Fruit peels, especially oranges, serve as organic pesticides for our local crops.

Interplanetary Power

We use every possible method: solar for houses, hydroelectric for water bodies, and wind turbines. But our core solution is Nuclear Energy. Just two plants power the entire Sanicon Valley.

Located in low-population areas, these plants provide the massive energy required for our heavy AI infrastructure, ensuring a zero-carbon footprint across the grid.

Urban Matrix

Every home features a backyard bot and an optional household assistant for simulated visitor interactions. Our strict zoning laws cap new homes at 3,000 sq ft to maximize space for energy plants, schools, and parks.

Manual labor is fully automated. Offices are arranged in rectangles around social squares, fostering spontaneous communication and the birth of better ideas. This is the blueprint for the interplanetary future.
